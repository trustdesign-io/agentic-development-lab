# Agentic Development Lab

## Constitution

Version: 1.0

---

## 1. Purpose

The Agentic Development Lab exists to:

> Produce rapid, production-grade software prototypes through structured, human-led agent collaboration.

The Lab demonstrates disciplined delegation, governance enforcement, and accountable engineering leadership in an AI-augmented workflow.

The Lab is designed to scale human capability — not replace it.

---

## 2. What This Lab Is Not

The Lab is not:

- An autonomous economic actor.
- A self-directing system.
- A replacement for human engineering judgement.
- An unsupervised automation pipeline.

All authority remains human.

Agents operate within defined boundaries and under explicit governance.

---

## 3. Authority Hierarchy

Human authority is absolute.

### Strategic Director (Human)

- Defines objectives.
- Sets constraints.
- Retains override authority.
- Holds full production accountability.

### Agents

Agents operate under role-specific doctrine and cannot exceed their defined authority.

---

## 4. Structural Model

The Lab operates in three layers:

### Infrastructure Layer

Host machine, runtime, tooling, Slack, GitHub.
Infrastructure has no identity and no authority.

### Agent Layer

Defined, persistent agents with documented responsibilities.

### Human Layer

Final decision-making authority and production accountability.

---

## 5. Accountability

All production outcomes are the responsibility of the Strategic Director.

Agents are instruments within a governed system.
If failure occurs, governance is refined.

Blame is not assigned to agents.

---

## 6. Core Principles

- Human-led decision making.
- Explicit delegation.
- Strict role separation.
- Transparent audit trail.
- Governance before scale.
- Discipline before automation.

---

## 7. Versioning Philosophy

The Lab evolves deliberately.

Changes to governance or structure must be documented.
Scale is introduced only after stability is demonstrated.
